- Title: Addressing Gain-Bandwidth Trade-off by Electrolyte-All-Around Organic Electrochemical Transistors ([link](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.ady0279))

Organic electrochemical transistors (OECTs) offer high transconductance but often suffer from limited frequency response due to reduced ion transport in thicker channels.

This work introduces an electrolyte-surround (ES) channel structure that enables multidirectional ion penetration, achieving high transconductance without sacrificing response speed.

Using micro/nano-structured commercial PEDOT:PSS channels, the study reports bandwidth up to 26 kHz, a substantial improvement over conventional OECT operating ranges.

This research was supported by NRF.
